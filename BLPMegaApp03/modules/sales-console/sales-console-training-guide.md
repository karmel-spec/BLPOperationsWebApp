# BLP Sales Console Quick Reference

## What To Work First

Start in the Inbox and work leads in this order:

1. **OUR TURN** or **FIRST CONTACT**
2. **Hot 8+**
3. **Brigham's Queue**
4. **Lonely**
5. **30 Days +**
6. Snoozed leads that have woken back up

## Action Column

**Temp**: 1-10 heat score. 8+ should usually be worked today.

**State**: engagement status. This tells you who has the ball.

**Last**: last known direction of contact.

**Next**: recommended next channel. The app should not recommend email if no email is on file, or text if no cell/phone is on file.

**Touches**: outbound -> inbound count. The parentheses show call/text/email totals.

## Symbols

`🔥` Hot or high temperature.

`❄️` Cooling or stale.

`⚠️` Needs Brigham, or the customer is waiting on us.

`🚨 Lonely` No meaningful contact for too long.

`📥` Inbound reply was logged.

`💤` Snoozed or inactive.

`✎` Edit the AI draft.

`↻` Regenerate the draft.

`←` Customer contacted us.

`→` We contacted the customer.

## Funnel

The funnel boxes are work queues. Click a box such as `Brand New`, `First Week`, or `30 Days +` to populate the Inbox with only those leads.

The aging report boxes work the same way.

## Lead Drawer

Click any row to open the lead drawer. The editable fields include:

Rep, Lead Name, Headline, Timeline, Next Action, Email, and Cell/Phone.

Use this drawer when the row is missing key details or the AI draft is using stale context.

## Draft Rules

Email drafts should send from `info@brighamlarsonpianos.com` once the Gmail backend is connected.

Video links should be shown as clean clickable titles instead of long URLs.

Calendly links should appear as `schedule me here`.

Piano brand names such as Young Chang, Hailun, Steinway, Yamaha, Kawai, Baldwin, Bösendorfer, Bechstein, and Mason & Hamlin should be capitalized in subject lines and message bodies.

Brigham should not refer to a customer as a “sales lead” in customer-facing language.
